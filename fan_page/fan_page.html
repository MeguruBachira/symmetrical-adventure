My Favorite Artist: Lil Uzi Vert

Childhood

Symes Basil Woods or better known by his stage name as Lil Uzi Vert was born on July 31st, 1994, in Philadelphia PA. Symes lived a normal childhood, he went to school made friends and was learning. Once he entered his teenage years he would discover his biggest inspiration, the man's name was Marilyn Manson. Marilyn Manson was an american songwriter and young Symes was immediately inspired by him and he single-handedly gets all of Symes inspiration, but he wouldn't start rapping just yet. He would drop out of school and get a job at a store called "Bottom Dollar". He didnt even make it through a week, he quit after four days and would then be kicked out of the house by his mom and under the name "Sealab Vertical" his original rapper name he would get his first tatto on his face to start his career. The word he picked: Faith, he picked that word to prove his faith to the rap industry.

Early Rapping

After Symes dropped out of 10th grade he would join rap groups like Steaktown and said he would go on to say "I'm just doing it for the money". After some time as he said in a interview, "Some guy was like, "You rap fast, man. Like a little machine gun." From that moment on I was Lil Uzi." He would go to work on his first project called: Purple Thoughtz Vol. 1, and it was a massive success and would blow up to the point were rap names like ASAP Mob heard it. It would continue to grow and it caught the attention of Def Ham A&R Don Cannon. They would sign Symes and take him to Academy Imprint and help produce his first mixtape named, The Real Uzi .It was featured alongside with rap names like Rich the Kid and ASAP Ferg on Carnage's single "WDYM". This didn't phase Symes though, he would continue posting songs to SoundCloud some of them being, No Wait, Pressure, and was later featured on Fall Out Boy and Wiz Khalifa's "Boys of Zummer". He was named "The Breakout Artist of 2015".

Rapping

After the award he would continue to make music making one of his most notorious songs "Money Longer" which would peak number 54 and is certified two times Platinum in the United States. Then on July 12, 2016 he released his fourth moxtape with songs like "Seven Million", featuring Future and "Erase Your Social" which debuted at number 55 on the Billboard 200 album chart and is certified Gold by the RIAA. Afterwards, Uzi would get a feature on "Bad and Boujee" which released August 31, 2016 and would go to and peak at number one on the US BillBoard Hot 100 for the week of January 21, 2017, making it Symes first single to make number one and would go 4x Platinum by the RIAA. Symes would then to make his next mixtape "Luv Is Rage 2", it was well received with Kingdom Hearts themed instrumentals and lyrics refferring to the show "Steven Universe" and hentai. This is when he was affiliated with emo-rap because of the song "XO Tour Llif3" because of the threats of suicide and depression in the song. It would start as number 49 on the Billboard and would peak at number 7, becoming Lil Uzi's highest charting song and biggest becoming 7x Platinum.

More Recently

He would make "Eternal Atake" having songs like "Silly Watch, Fustsal Shuffle, Homecoming", and many more hits. It was supposed to come out in 2018 after making a tweet saying "Eternal Atake" but after many delays it would come out on March 6th, 2020. While Eternal Atake was being delayed he would make singles like "Sanguine Paradise" and "That's a Rack" and on December 13, 2019 he would drop "Futsal Shuffle 2020" which was the lead single of the entire album, and would then release "That Way" five days before the album dropped. Eternal Atake would debut on the number 1 spot for Billboard 200 and Uzi would tease the sequel to his 2016 mixtape featuring many artists including 21 Savage, Lil Durk, Chief Keef, and more. On July 10th, 2020 he would appear on a remix of StaySolidRocky's "Party Girl" then eleven days later would hint at a project called "Pluto x Baby Pluto". It would release on November 13th, 2020 and would debute at number two on the US Billboard 200 and would become his 3rd album to be a US top ten.

Awards

Lil Uzi has been nominated for many awards like MTV Video Music Awards, Billboard Music Awards, Grammy Awards, iHeartRadio Music Awards, and American Music Awards. The only one he’s won this far is the “Song of Summer” for the MTV Video Music Awards which was with the song “XO Tour Llif3” back in 2015.
